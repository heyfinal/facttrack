Section V

Methodology & Scope of Examination

Per Texas land-services practice, scope and exceptions are stated here in lieu of a separate certification page.

Examination scope

Records examined
Anderson County Official Public Records as indexed on publicsearch.us; Texas Railroad Commission EWA wellbore data (not yet pulled).
Period
1957-10-28 – 1969-10-17
Examiner
FactTrack rule engine, version 0.1.0 (8 registered rules)
Clause extraction
Tesseract OCR (PSM 6) over signed publicsearch.us page images; regex extraction of primary term, royalty fraction, Pugh-clause language, depth limit, continuous-development language, and deceased-lessor markers. Coverage on examined leases: 85%.

Records expressly excluded from this examination

  • County District Clerk probate dockets. Probate is filed separately from deed records and is not indexed on publicsearch.us. Probate-gap findings (rule r02) should be confirmed against the relevant District Clerk's docket before recording any curative instrument.
  • County Appraisal District (CAD) records. Current mineral- and surface-owner identification has not been cross-referenced.
  • Texas RRC monthly production (PR) reports and W-2 completion records. Primary-term + continuous-production findings (rule r05) rely on absence of recorded release; an offsetting release elsewhere in the chain should be confirmed before treating the finding as actionable.
  • Texas General Land Office state-leased mineral records. Tracts with GLO interests are not currently surfaced.
  • Recorded division orders and JOAs. NRI reconciliation is presented as best-available; division-order accuracy is not represented.

Data sources actually populated in this run

  • Anderson County OPR (publicsearch.us) — Lease, assignment, release, AOH, probate index entries — 32 rows ingested (last pulled 2026-05-13).

Statutory references applied

  • Texas Estates Code §203.001. An Affidavit of Heirship on file in deed records for five or more years is prima facie evidence of heirship. r02_probate_gap downgrades to medium severity when an AOH is on file but younger than five years, and does not fire when an AOH ≥ five years old or any probate / RoP instrument names the same decedent.
  • Habendum-clause defeasance. r05_primary_term_no_continuous_prod does not fire when a grantor-side reverse-search has located a recorded release of the lease by the lessee; the lease died honestly and the chain is clean.

Standards & limitations

Findings are generated by a deterministic rule engine evaluating real public-record data. Each finding has a confidence score reflecting data-quality assumptions; values below 0.50 are flagged for manual review. Curative-effort estimates are based on East-Texas operator pricing as of 2026-05-13 21:17 UTC and are guidance only — actual cost depends on document discovery, recording fees, and any required attorney involvement. This report is a triage instrument and does not constitute a title opinion under the Texas State Bar's Title Examination Standards. A licensed Texas attorney should review any curative instrument prior to recording.
